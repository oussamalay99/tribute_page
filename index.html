Rachid Yazami

Moroccan french engineer and scientist

Rachid Yazami, Moroccan, French research scientist, consultant, editor. His achievements include invention of carbon-lithium anode for lithium-ion batteries, carbon fluoride cathode for 3V Lithium batteries and 5V rechargeable batteries, and the electrochemical thermodynamics measurements system.

Here is a timeline for Mr. Rachid Yazami:

  • 1953: Rachid Yazami was born on april 16th in Fez.
  • 1972: He arrived in France.
  • 1978: Electrochemistry enginnering degree at Institute National Polytech. Grenoble, France.
  • 1980: The first one to establish the lithium intercalation into graphite in an electrochemical cell. This discovery led to create lithium-graphite anode used in lithium ion batteries nowadays.
  • 1985: Doctor of Philosophy in Electrochemistry, Institute National Polytech. Grenoble, France.
  • 1985: Rachid Yazami joined the French National Center for Scientific Research (CNRS) as research associate.
  • 1998: He promoted to research director et CNRS.
  • 2000-2010: Visiting associate at California Institute of Technology where he conducted cooperative research in electrode materials.
  • 2007: He founded a start-up company in California to develop and commercialize his discoveries.
  • 2010-2012: He was appointed a Nanyang Visiting Professor then he was promoted in 2012 the Cheng Tsang Man Chair Professor in Energy at the School of Materials Science and Engineering of the Nanyang Technological University (NTU) in Singapore.
  • 2011: In 2011 Yazami founded a new start-up company in Singapore, KVI,PTE LTD, which develops and commercialises novel equipments and components to investigate novel battery materials and batteries for enhanced energy, power and cycle life performances and also to increase their safety.
  • 2014: Rachid Yazami, John Goodenough, Yoshio Nishi and Akira Yoshino were awarded the Draper Prize by The National Academy of Engineering for pioneering and leading the groundwork for today’s lithium ion battery. The prize, which was then in its 25th year, includes a $500,000 award. He was also a finalist of the Global Energy Award (Russia, 2014). In the same year, Yazami received the Royal Medal (Wissam Malaki) of Intellectual Competency from HM the King of Morocco Mohamed VI
  • 2016: Rachid Yazami received an award as a finalist of the Marius Lavet Prize of Inventing-Engineers, Paris, France. He was awarded also the title of Chevalier de la legion d'honneur of France.
  • 2017: He received the Honors Award of the Moroccans of the World.
  • 2018: He promYazami was the recipient of the Takreem Award for Science and Technological Achievement considered as the Arab Scientist of the year.
  • 2019: Yazami received the Arab Investor Award in the “Green Application” category.
  • 2020: Yazami won the Mohammed bin Rashid Medal for Scientific Excellence in the UAE

Even his efforts in realizing his co-invention of the lithium-ion battery which was honoured with the 2019 Noble Prize in Chemistry, Yazami was unfortunately ommited from the award due to Noble Prize's limitation of up to three recipients which was given to his colleagues Stanley Whittingham, John Goodenough and Akira Yoshino.
